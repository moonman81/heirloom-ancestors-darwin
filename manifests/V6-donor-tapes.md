# V6 (Version 6 Unix, Bell Labs, 1975) — donor tapes

V6 is the direct ancestor of V7, immortalised by John Lions' commentary
(1977). Four independent donor tapes preserved on TUHS provide
cross-verification for the V6 source.

## Location on TUHS Archive

- `https://www.tuhs.org/Archive/Distributions/Research/Collinson_v6/`
  (Peter Collinson's tape)
- `https://www.tuhs.org/Archive/Distributions/Research/Ken_Wellsch_v6/`
  (Ken Wellsch's tape)
- `https://www.tuhs.org/Archive/Distributions/Research/Tim_Shoppa_v6/`
  (Tim Shoppa's tape — see also `Applications/Shoppa_Tapes/`)
- `https://www.tuhs.org/Archive/Distributions/Research/Utah_v4/`
  (Utah V4 — related earlier release)
- `https://www.tuhs.org/Archive/Recordings/1975_Unix_Code_Walkthru/Eric_Allman_v6.tar.gz`
  (Eric Allman's own V6 tape from the 1975 code walkthrough)

## Content

V6 contains the pre-Bourne-shell `/bin/sh` (Thompson shell), the
earliest full-featured awk-precursor tools, and the whole userland
that Lions annotated. Every heirloom-*-darwin utility ultimately
traces here.

## Licensing

Caldera 2002 Ancient UNIX licence grant covers V1-V7 including V6.

## Why this matters to Heirloom

Bibliographically foundational; annotates the point immediately
before the Bourne shell was written (V6 → V7 = Bourne shell
introduction). Complements the V7 manifest.
